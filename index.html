New Technologies

How could they impact us?

Games

As humanity has advanced, so has our entertainment industry. Games have always been a central part of our lives and they passively and actively change our mindset. They have the power to simulate places we cannot visit in real-life or situations we can only speculate could happen in the future. This means games could help day-to-day people understand complex things such as disease epidemics through a simulator, prompting them to maintain good hygiene habits. On the flip side, it could also increase violent tendencies through shooting games that normalize intense, gory violence, meaning that games for pleasure could normalize tragedies and possibly encourage them to happen.

Deepfakes

Deepfakes threaten our perception of reality and security around the world. When you can’t confidently tell apart a real picture or video from a fake one, distrusts starts to run rapid among society because anyone could be lying. This is extremely dangerous for governments because deepfakes of politicians can ruin careers and destabilize countries when citizens don’t know who to trust, leading to power struggles.

Is this video real?

AR/VR

AR/VR is an emerging technology that can radically change how we interact with technology. It lets us fully immerse ourselves into a realm of possibilities that is unlimited, as long as we can make it. It can help doctors find new ways of treating patients without harming them, pilots can practice without a real aircraft, and it can help kids with disabilities experience things that they otherwise would not be able to do. People, however, could also use it to get experiences in illegal activities, which will make maintaining safety more difficult because it will have to adapt to this new technology training people to harm others.

Histogram

Histograms in image editing allow images to be sharper, clearer, and more pleasing to those who view the image. It helps even out colors and brightness to make it more like how we view it in real life.

Recent Projects

Social Media Interview

Interviews can make anyone nervous, no matter if you’re the one interviewing the other person. In this interview, It dealt with the effect of social media on people and our perception of the world. Social media can change how we view news, people and products because social media is meant to show you a specific narrative, not necessarily reality. This led to a discussion on how we have to be very careful in believing what we see because it may not represent all parts of the story. Adding background music and clearing up noise made this a bit harder than expected but it delivered an easy to follow end result.

My Podcast

Making a podcast sounds easy and intimidating at the same time. From worries about a topic, to how your voice sounds, to what platform you’ll use, it makes starting one very difficult. In this podcast, the topic was social media and changes in advertising caused by the prevalence and easy accessibility of social apps. Advertising changed from showing what they had to offer and consumers choosing from that selection, to showing that they would cater to the consumers wants and needs. It led to a more personal approach in reaching audiences and communicating with customers, to increase their influence base.

Digital Storytelling

Life happens. It’s a common phrase that emphasizes that we can’t control what goes on in our life. We can, however, embrace it and move on, keeping memories as stories to tell later. In this project, a video was made by putting together pictures, video and music with a voice over explaining the impact of one of my most recent trips. It had to be woven together in order to project the feelings and lesson of the trip, which was a bit difficult when you consider time constraints, limited knowledge and having to have a clear and concise story that engages the viewers.
